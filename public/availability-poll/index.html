A modern take on the availability grid

If you've used a group-availability grid before, Huddlr will feel familiar — with a few upgrades that matter.

  • Built for mobile. Painting availability works with a thumb, not just a mouse — so the people you invite can actually respond from their phones.
  • Automatic timezones. Everyone marks time in their own zone, so a cross-country or international group lands on a slot that's actually fair.
  • No sign-up, no cost. Share a link and people respond — no accounts, no ads to click past, nothing to pay for.

Availability poll questions

What's the difference between this and a time-slot poll?

An availability poll lets people mark all the times they're free across a range, which is ideal when you have no fixed options yet. If you already have two or three specific slots in mind, a meeting-time poll is faster.

Do respondents need an account?

No. Anyone with the link can fill in their availability without signing up or logging in.

Does it handle different timezones?

Yes. Each person paints and reads the grid in their local timezone, so remote and international groups don't have to convert times by hand.
